Interview Tips

The following provides basic guidelines on how to approach an upcoming interview. Especially tailored towards co-op job interviews, the main 12 points are listed below in the step by step manner in which they should be followed.


  1. Research the company and know it well
  2. Practice common interview questions - Glassdoor
  3. Prepare solid questions for interviewer - on the company, their experience, a challenging aspect
  4. Carry professional portfolio with copies of updated resume, references, opt: recommendations
  5. Always dress your best in suit and heels/suit and tie
  6. Be on time for the interview - scope out company location before your interview date
  7. Maintain confident body language - strong hand shakes, eye contact, sit upright
  8. Answer only the questions asked, and be mindful of using appropriate examples
  9. Always maintain professionalism in everything
  10. Make a good first impression and be memorable
  11. Ask for interviewer’s business card to follow-up later
  12. Send a thank you email to all interviews

Sample Questions

Here are some sample questions and tips associated with them that can you help you prepare.
  • Why should I hire you?
    • This can be one of the most challenging to easy questions depending on the job. The main key here is to make yourself look unique and highlight points(soft skills or hard skills) that you sets you apart!
  • Do you have any questions for us?
    • Believe it or not, companies do care about this question. This is not only a way for them to assess your interest but also understand your thinking process and your priorities in a work place.
  • Where else are you interviewing?
    • While not the most common one, when asked this can get tricky as there is no way to predict where your answer might lead. The best option would be to stay prepared.
  • Give an example of a time when you didn't have enough information to do you job? What steps do you take?
    • While thise question might seem to specific, these type questions are mainly situational based. It is always good to have a few examples in general at the back of your mind and mold it the question like in this case.
